Polish–Ukrainian Conflict in Modern Days (1991–Today)

Overview

Both states demonstrate how historical issues can become political priorities particularly after gaining wider public attention, suggesting that political calculations, domestic considerations, and public expectations may sometimes influence approaches alongside a genuine search for justice and reconciliation.

On the Polish side, this dynamic can be observed in the significant time gap between the early post-independence period and the wider recognition of the Volhynia massacres following the publication of the works of Władysław Siemaszko and Ewa Siemaszko in 2000. The limited institutional attention during the years between 1992 and 2000 raises questions about whether historical issues receive sustained state attention primarily when they become politically significant and publicly visible.

A similar pattern can be observed on the Ukrainian side in the relatively recent approach toward certain historical exhumation requests. The limited progress over previous decades, followed by renewed activity in specific circumstances, raises questions about whether practical cooperation is pursued consistently or whether it becomes more likely when there is a clear political benefit or broader strategic interest involved.

At the same time, Ukraine continues to promote elements of a national historical narrative in which figures and organizations connected to the Organization of Ukrainian Nationalists (OUN-B) and Ukrainian Insurgent Army (UPA) are often presented primarily as symbols of national struggle, while a more critical examination of crimes committed by these organizations remains limited and politically sensitive. This remains one of the most divisive historical issues between Poland and Ukraine and continues to attract criticism from some external observers who emphasize the importance of confronting difficult and controversial aspects of national history.

In this sense, the issue is not only whether historical responsibility is acknowledged, but whether governments are willing to act when doing so carries political costs rather than political benefits. The pattern observed on both sides suggests that when historical issues do not provide immediate political advantage, the suffering of victims can become secondary to political calculations. As a result, victims and their families risk being neglected by the very states that claim to preserve their historical memory. Political interests may take priority over remembrance and justice, leaving unresolved tragedies sidelined for years and allowing the needs of victims to become subordinate to contemporary state objectives.

Ultimately, the tragedy is that political struggles on both sides continue to develop around the memory of victims of horrific crimes. Instead of allowing historical truth and remembrance to serve as a foundation for reconciliation, the suffering of the past is too often transformed into an instrument of modern political conflict. When historical memory becomes primarily a matter of national interests rather than a commitment to truth and dignity for victims, reconciliation becomes significantly more difficult.

Main Events (1997–Now)

Ukraine Poland Joint 3rd parties
21 May 1997 Joint
▶ Joint declaration "On Understanding and Reconciliation"

Presidents Aleksander Kwaśniewski and Leonid Kuczma sign a document in Kyiv calling for mutual forgiveness of past wrongs and a dialogue based on historical truth.

2000 3rd parties
▶ The Siemaszko monograph

Publication of Władysław and Ewa Siemaszko's two-volume "Genocide committed by Ukrainian nationalists against the Polish population of Volhynia 1939–1945" becomes a key reference point for Polish research into the scale of the crime (approx. 60,000 victims in Volhynia alone).

2000 3rd parties
▶ Sympathy survey in Ukraine

A survey conducted in Ukraine found that Poles ranked third in the hierarchy of national sympathies, behind Belarus (1st) and Russia (2nd).

11 July 2003 Joint
▶ 60th anniversary commemoration at Pawlivka

Presidents Kwaśniewski and Kuczma unveil a reconciliation monument at Pawlivka; the Polish president explicitly called the events genocide, while the Ukrainian side maintained a narrative of "symmetrical guilt".

11 July 2003 3rd parties
▶ CBOS survey on Poles' knowledge

A CBOS poll conducted around the same time found that 44% of Poles could not identify either the victims or the perpetrators of the Volhynia massacre.

12 October 2007 Ukraine
▶ Roman Shukhevych proclaimed Hero of Ukraine

President Viktor Yushchenko posthumously honours the UPA commander, provoking sharp protests in Poland over his role in the Volhynia massacre.

28 February 2009 Joint
▶ Joint commemoration at Huta Pieniacka

Presidents Lech Kaczyński and Viktor Yushchenko pay tribute to Poles murdered by Ukrainian units, though the term "genocide" is avoided in the speeches.

15 July 2009 Poland
▶ Sejm resolution on the "hallmarks of genocide"

The Polish parliament adopts by acclamation a document describing the Volhynia crime as a murder of an ethnic-cleansing character bearing the hallmarks of genocide.

2008 3rd parties
▶ A deepening knowledge crisis in Poland

A further CBOS survey shows worsening historical awareness: 54% of Poles no longer knew who the victims of the Volhynia massacre were, and 57% did not know who the perpetrators were.

22 January 2010 Ukraine
▶ Stepan Bandera proclaimed Hero of Ukraine

President Yushchenko's controversial decision to honour the OUN leader provokes consternation and official protests from Polish authorities.

25 February 2010 3rd parties
▶ European Parliament resolution on Bandera

MEPs express regret over the awarding of the national hero title to Bandera and call on Ukraine's new authorities to reverse the decision in the name of European values.

2 April 2011 Ukraine
▶ Ukrainian court revokes the Hero of Ukraine titles

Ukraine's Supreme Administrative Court upholds the annulment of Yushchenko's decrees regarding Bandera and Shukhevych, temporarily easing the diplomatic dispute.

11 July 2013 Poland
▶ 70th anniversary and acknowledgment of "exceptional cruelty"

The Sejm and Senate adopt resolutions describing the crime as an "ethnic cleansing bearing the hallmarks of genocide", mentioning for the first time the exceptional cruelty of the perpetrators.

9 April 2015 Ukraine
▶ Ukrainian law grants combatant status to OUN-UPA

The Verkhovna Rada grants combatant status to members of OUN and UPA and bans questioning the legitimacy of their struggle, just hours after President Komorowski's conciliatory speech in Kyiv — seen in Poland as a slap in the face.

8 July 2016 Ukraine
▶ President Poroshenko's symbolic gesture in Warsaw

The Ukrainian president kneels before the monument to the victims of the Volhynia massacre in Warsaw's Żoliborz district — one of the most important, though little-publicised, gestures of reconciliation.

22 July 2016 Poland
▶ Establishment of the National Day of Remembrance for Genocide Victims

The Sejm designates 11 July as a day of remembrance for the victims of crimes committed by Ukrainian nationalists, met with "deep concern" from the Ukrainian parliament.

7 October 2016 3rd parties
▶ Premiere of Wojciech Smarzowski's film "Wołyń"

The film causes an enormous public reaction in Poland and is banned in Ukraine, sharpening mutual perceptions of this history.

20 October 2016 3rd parties
▶ Joint Declaration of Memory and Solidarity

The parliaments of Poland, Ukraine and Lithuania jointly condemn totalitarian regimes and hold the USSR responsible for the outbreak of the Second World War.

April 2017 Poland
▶ Dismantling of the Hruszowice UPA monument

A monument honoring UPA fighters, erected in Hruszowice in 1994 and long described by Polish authorities as unauthorized, is dismantled by local Polish authorities. Ukraine claims UPA fighters were buried beneath it.

April 2017 Ukraine
▶ Ukrainian moratorium on exhumations

In response to the dismantling of the Hruszowice monument, Kyiv bans search and exhumation work for Polish specialists.

26 January 2018 Poland
▶ Dispute over the amended IPN law

The introduction of provisions on "crimes of Ukrainian nationalists" leads to a sharp diplomatic crisis and the suspension of the Polish-Ukrainian Historians' Forum, though some provisions are later ruled imprecise.

June 2018 Poland
▶ Repeal of criminal penalties in the IPN law

Following significant diplomatic pressure from Ukraine, Israel and the United States, Poland repeals the criminal penalties introduced in the January 2018 amendment, though civil liability for denying the crimes remains a possibility.

2018 Poland
▶ IPN archaeological research at the Hruszowice site

Poland's Institute of National Remembrance (IPN) conducts archaeological research at the former monument site and concludes it found no evidence of UPA graves at that exact location, weakening the Ukrainian argument that the monument was directly protecting a burial site.

8 July 2018 Joint
▶ Separate 75th-anniversary commemorations

President Duda attends ceremonies in Lutsk while President Poroshenko attends events in Sahryń at the same time, exposing the lack of a shared vision for the anniversary.

February 2019 3rd parties
▶ CBOS survey on sympathy towards Ukrainians

After a period of tension, a rise in Polish sympathy toward Ukrainians is recorded (31% versus 24% a year earlier), linked to softened historical rhetoric.

19-21 January 2022 Joint
▶ Talks in Wisła and a "tactical truce"

Presidents Duda and Zelensky agree that Poland would refrain from condemning the entire Ukrainian national movement in exchange for curbing the cult of the perpetrators of the Volhynia massacre (Shukhevych, Savur).

2022 3rd parties
▶ EU "Solidarity Lanes" opened for Ukrainian grain

After Russia's February 2022 invasion, naval blockades and attacks disrupt Ukraine's Black Sea grain export routes. The EU establishes "Solidarity Lanes" to help Ukraine export agricultural products through neighbouring countries, including Poland. Much of the grain is meant to transit onward to Africa and the Middle East, but some remains in Poland and other neighbouring states because of logistical problems.

Late 2022 – early 2023 Joint
▶ Growing tensions among farmers over Ukrainian grain

Polish farmers complain that Ukrainian grain is depressing local prices, arguing lower Ukrainian production costs leave them unable to sell their own harvest profitably; the government faces mounting pressure from rural communities ahead of the 2023 parliamentary elections. Poland argues it opened its market to help Ukraine but that protecting Polish agriculture is now necessary, while Ukraine argues grain exports are vital for its economy and wartime survival, and sees restrictions as damaging one of its main sources of revenue.

February 2023 Ukraine
▶ Controversial naming of a military unit after Shukhevych

The naming of the 10th Mountain Assault Brigade "Edelweiss" evokes negative historical associations in Poland and raises questions about continuity with collaborationist formations.

April 2023 Poland
▶ Poland bans imports of Ukrainian grain

Poland announces a ban on imports of Ukrainian grain and several other agricultural products; Hungary and other neighbouring countries introduce similar measures. The European Commission initially criticises the unilateral national bans, as trade policy is an EU responsibility.

5 April 2023 Joint
▶ Zelenskyy awarded the Order of the White Eagle

Volodymyr Zelenskyy receives Poland's highest state honour, the Order of the White Eagle, during an official visit to Poland.

May 2023 3rd parties
▶ CBOS survey on an expected apology

A CBOS poll finds that 78% of Poles believe President Zelensky should apologise for Volhynia and 72% describe the events unambiguously as genocide.

May 2023 Joint
▶ Diplomatic exchange over an apology

Polish Foreign Ministry spokesman Łukasz Jasina's suggestion that Ukraine should apologise is called "unacceptable and unfortunate" by Ukrainian ambassador Vasyl Zvarych.

May-September 2023 3rd parties
▶ EU compromise on temporary grain restrictions

The European Commission introduces temporary restrictions on Ukrainian wheat, maize, rapeseed and sunflower seed imports into Poland, Bulgaria, Hungary, Romania and Slovakia, while transit through these countries remains possible, aiming to reduce pressure on local markets while preserving Ukraine's export routes.

9 July 2023 Joint
▶ Joint prayer in Lutsk Cathedral

On the 80th anniversary, Presidents Duda and Zelensky take part in an ecumenical service honouring the "innocent victims of Volhynia".

July 2023 Poland
▶ Dispute over medals and "pitchfork politics"

During the 80th-anniversary commemorations, President Duda criticises circles demanding radical action, triggering a sharp dispute with Kresy activists; political calls to strip Zelensky of the Order of the White Eagle are also raised in parliamentary debate.

11 July 2023 Poland
▶ Unanimous Sejm resolution for the 80th anniversary

The Polish parliament calls on Ukraine to acknowledge guilt, stressing that reconciliation must include the exhumation of all victims of the crime, and uses the term "genocide" five times.

September 2023 Joint
▶ Ban extension refused; Ukraine files WTO complaint

The European Commission decides not to extend the temporary restrictions, but Poland, Hungary and Slovakia continue their own national bans on Ukrainian grain. Ukraine responds by filing a complaint at the World Trade Organization against Poland and the other countries. Poland argues it is defending its farmers, while Ukraine argues that Poland, despite being an ally, is restricting a crucial wartime export sector.

September 2023 3rd parties
▶ Scandal in the Canadian Parliament

The honouring of SS Galizien veteran Yaroslav Hunka draws international condemnation, including from Poland and Jewish organisations, and recalls the unresolved role of Ukrainian collaborationist units.

2024 3rd parties
▶ Sharp decline in Ukrainian sympathy toward Poles

A public opinion poll records a fall in positive sentiment toward Poles among Ukrainians from 83% in 2022 to 41% in 2024, attributed largely to the "grain crisis" and border blockades.

2024 3rd parties
▶ Ukrainian survey on exhumations

Only a third of Ukrainians surveyed support the unconditional unblocking of exhumation work, while 40% believe it should be contingent on Poland meeting specific conditions.

16 May 2024 3rd parties
▶ Germany deports seven Ukrainian soldiers for wearing Nazi symbols

German authorities deport seven Ukrainian soldiers undergoing training in Germany after they are found wearing Nazi and SS-linked tattoos and insignia, reviving concerns about far-right symbolism within Ukrainian military ranks.

26 November 2024 Joint
▶ Sikorski-Sybiha declaration in Warsaw

The foreign ministers declare there are no formal obstacles to exhumations and express readiness to establish a joint historical working group.

11 July 2025 Poland
▶ First official national commemoration under the new law

Poland commemorates the victims of the Volhynia massacre under legislation signed by the president in June 2025.

12 July 2025 Poland
▶ Nawrocki calls for full-scale exhumations at Chełm

President-elect Karol Nawrocki calls for full-scale exhumations during a commemoration in Chełm, asserting that historical truth is a prerequisite for a true partnership against Russian neo-imperialism.

16 July 2025 3rd parties
▶ Lublin Triangle historian platform announced

Within the framework of the Lublin Triangle, a new platform for historians is announced to facilitate research and dialogue on contentious historical grievances.

March 2026 Ukraine
▶ Official working structure to design the project

A high-level meeting - Presidential Office, parliament, government and historians - is held to design the project. It explicitly included OUN figures.

May 2026 Joint
▶ Major Polish-Ukrainian historical conference

The initiative aims to create a permanent, professional platform for dialogue between experts from both countries, independent of current political cycles. No major conclusions were reached, and the Polish IPN was not included in the talks.

May 2026 Ukraine
▶ Reburial of Andriy Melnyk

Ukraine carries out the reburial of Andriy Melnyk, leader of OUN-M, and his wife at the National Military Memorial Cemetery.

26 May 2026 Ukraine
▶ Military unit named "Heroes of the UPA"

President Zelenskyy officially gives a Ukrainian military unit the honorary name "Heroes of the UPA".

29 May 2026 Poland
▶ Nawrocki announces intent to revoke the Order

Senior Polish officials and institutions respond publicly: President Karol Nawrocki announces he will seek to revoke the Order of the White Eagle previously awarded to Zelenskyy.

19 June 2026 Poland
▶ Zelenskyy stripped of the Order of the White Eagle

Poland strips Volodymyr Zelenskyy of the Order of the White Eagle, the honour he had received during his April 2023 visit.

20 June 2026 Ukraine
▶ Zelenskyy returns the Order via Nova Poshta

Zelenskyy sends the Order of the White Eagle back to President Nawrocki via the standard shipping branch Nova Poshta, an act viewed as a major diplomatic snub.

20 June 2026 Ukraine
▶ Mass return of Polish honours in solidarity

Prominent Ukrainian figures, including former presidents Kuchma, Yushchenko and Poroshenko, as well as Kyrylo Budanov, announce they will give up their Polish state honours in solidarity with Zelenskyy.

25-26 June 2026 Ukraine
▶ Zelenskyy skips the Ukraine Recovery Conference in Gdańsk

Zelenskyy refuses to attend the Ukraine Recovery Conference in Gdańsk, sending a lower-level delegation led by the Prime Minister instead.

28 June 2026 Ukraine
▶ Draft law on the Ukrainian National Pantheon submitted

A draft law on the Ukrainian National Pantheon is submitted to parliament.

30 June 2026 Poland
▶ Polish warnings over Ukraine's EU accession

Officials suggest Ukraine's membership could be blocked until the "cult of Bandera" and the truth about Volhynia are addressed in line with European values.

July 2026 Ukraine
▶ Verkhovna Rada establishes the Pantheon

The Verkhovna Rada adopts a law establishing the Pantheon as a state memorial system in Kyiv.

8 July 2026 3rd parties
▶ EU resolution criticizes Ukrainian historical policies

The European Union issues a resolution/statement criticizing Ukraine's historical policies, including the treatment of the Volhynia massacre and the commemoration of OUN-UPA figures.

July 2026 Ukraine
▶ Budanov: Ukraine will not accept an ultimatum from anyone

"Ukraine will not accept an ultimatum from anyone in this world. Recently it was Russia that tried to give us an ultimatum. No offense to Poland, but it is somewhat more powerful than Poland, and we still didn't accept it," says the head of the Office of the President of Ukraine, Kyrylo Budanov.

The Exhumation Paradox

The data reveal a troubling paradox: Ukraine finds ways to cooperate efficiently with Germany on historical issues, yet repeatedly delays and complicates exhumation efforts requested by Poland. The difference appears not to be a question of capability, but of priorities. This contrast suggests that exhumations concerning Polish victims have become less a matter of administrative procedure and more a matter of political will.

At times, these delays are defended by references to alleged procedural problems, safety concerns, or potential risks connected with the planned work. However, such explanations appear inconsistent when compared with Ukraine's cooperation with Germany on similar historical matters, where these obstacles do not seem to prevent progress. This raises concerns that arguments related to procedure or security may sometimes be applied selectively rather than representing genuine barriers.

Furthermore, the approval of certain excavation sites has also raised questions. In some cases, permissions appear to be limited to locations that are already known and documented, or to areas where the connection to the Volhynia massacre is unclear or indirect. This creates the impression that the selection of approved sites may not always reflect a comprehensive effort to identify and investigate the burial places of Polish victims, but rather a more restricted approach that limits the scope of historical clarification.
